What a free IELTS mock test really means

By Xavier, founder of ParrotTalk · July 30, 2026 · 3 min read

Is there a genuinely free IELTS mock test online, the full thing and not a two-question teaser? Yes. On ParrotTalk you can sit a complete IELTS mock test, all four sections one after another under real exam timing, and it is 100% free with no sign-up. This is the story of how that whole test came together, and why I think sitting the full thing matters.

For the first couple of weeks the site was really four separate practice rooms: Listening here, Reading there, Writing and Speaking in their own corners. Useful, but not the real experience. The exam is not four calm little exercises on four different afternoons. It is a long morning where fatigue from one section spills into the next.

Stitching four rooms into one exam

Around the middle of July I connected the four modules into a single continuous run, in the real order, each with its own clock. Listening first, then Reading, then Writing, then Speaking, and a single band summary at the end instead of four disconnected scores. Roughly thirty minutes of Listening, an hour of Reading, an hour of Writing, and about twelve minutes of Speaking. Plan for about three hours in one sitting, the way the real test asks of you.

Doing it in one go teaches you things a single section never will. You learn what your concentration does in the third hour. You learn to carry a steady pace instead of sprinting the first task and limping through the last. You get a band that reflects a full exam rather than your best isolated moment.

When three hours is too much today

Some days you do not have three hours, and I did not want that to be a reason to skip practice. So there is also a shorter route: a Quick Mock Test that still touches all four skills but fits in about fifty minutes, drawing from thousands of combinations so it rarely repeats. It is the same idea at a smaller scale, for the evening when you only have a window.

Both are free, and neither hides the good part behind a wall. When you are ready to sit the whole thing under exam conditions, the free mock test is right there. My only advice: treat it like the real morning. Phone away, timer honest, no restarting a section because you did not like the first minute.

The short version: a full IELTS mock test on ParrotTalk means all four sections, back to back, real timing, one final band. 100% free, no sign-up.
