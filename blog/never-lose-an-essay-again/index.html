Never lose an essay again

By Xavier, founder of ParrotTalk · August 6, 2026 · 3 min read

Want IELTS Writing Task 2 feedback without the quiet dread of losing a half-finished essay to a stray tab close or a phone that decides to reload? ParrotTalk now saves your writing as you type, so your draft is still there when you come back. It is a small feature, and it removes a genuinely large fear.

Writing is the section where a technical hiccup hurts the most. Nobody grieves a lost multiple-choice answer, you just click it again. But twenty minutes into a Task 2 essay, with your argument finally coming together, losing the lot to a browser reload is the kind of thing that makes a person close the tab and not come back for a week.

Saving quietly, in the background

So your essay now saves itself as you write, quietly, without a button to remember or a moment to interrupt your thinking. Leave and return, get bumped by a notification, switch from your phone to a laptop later in the day, and the words are waiting for you where you left them. It stays in your own browser, nowhere else, which is exactly where a private draft should live.

This is not a glamorous feature and it will never be the reason someone tells a friend about the site. It is a seatbelt. You do not think about it until the day it is the only thing standing between you and starting over from a blank page.

Less fear, better writing

There is a real payoff beyond convenience. When you are not subconsciously guarding against loss, you write more freely. You are willing to delete a weak paragraph and try again, because the safety net is there. Fear makes for cramped, cautious essays, and a little reassurance underneath the keyboard actually shows up in the work.

When your draft is ready, send it through the writing checker for a band and detailed notes, or start a fresh piece in the full Writing practice. Both are 100% free, no sign-up.

The short version: your IELTS essay now saves automatically as you type, so a reload or a closed tab never costs you your work. Free, no sign-up.
