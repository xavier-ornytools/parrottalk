❓ FAQ

Common Questions

What is ParrotTalk, exactly?

ParrotTalk is a free, independent IELTS practice simulator. It offers full mock tests for all four sections: Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking, with instant, AI-generated feedback and an estimated band score, so you can practice under realistic conditions before your real exam.

Is ParrotTalk affiliated with IELTS, the British Council, IDP, or Cambridge Assessment English?

No. ParrotTalk is an independent, unofficial preparation tool. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, sponsored by, or connected in any way to IELTS, the British Council, IDP: IELTS Australia, or Cambridge Assessment English. "IELTS" and related marks belong to their respective owners. We reference them only to describe the exam format we help you practice for. See our Terms of Use for the full statement.

Is the band score I receive an official IELTS result?

No, it's an AI-generated estimate, not an official IELTS score. A real IELTS result comes from certified human examiners following a strict marking framework. ParrotTalk's band is produced by an AI model scoring your answers against the same four official criteria examiners use. ⚠️ Word of caution: your estimated band may differ from an official result by roughly ±0.5 to 1 band: this is not a measured accuracy figure or a guarantee, just a heads-up on how much variance to expect. Use it as a practice signal to track your progress, not as proof of your level for a visa, university, or immigration application.

How is my Speaking test evaluated?

When you record your Speaking answers, your audio is sent to Google Gemini (an AI model) via our server, which scores you on the same four official IELTS Speaking criteria: Fluency & Coherence, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range & Accuracy, and Pronunciation. This process is fully automated. No human examiner reviews your answers. Pronunciation in particular is judged by AI listening to your actual recording, which is inherently more variable than a trained human ear. Before your first recording, ParrotTalk asks for your explicit consent to this processing.

Are my voice recordings stored?

No. Your audio is sent to our server, forwarded to Google Gemini for evaluation, and then discarded. It is not stored on our servers or retained afterward. Only your resulting band scores are kept (for 90 days) to build your practice history. Full details in our Privacy Policy.

Is ParrotTalk free?

Yes, currently free to use, with no subscription, credit card, or premium tier. ParrotTalk is a non-commercial beta project (see our Terms of Use), so this may evolve as it matures, but any change would be communicated clearly and would never retroactively affect scores or data already saved in your browser.

How reliable is the band score (can I trust it)?

We can't yet quote a measured precision figure for ParrotTalk's band estimates, and we won't invent one. Our goal is to calibrate scoring by comparing ParrotTalk's estimated bands against real, official IELTS Test Report Forms from beta users who complete both, and to publish that measured accuracy gap openly and transparently once we have enough real data, rather than assume it. Until then, treat every band as a directional practice estimate: useful for spotting weak areas and tracking progress, not a substitute for an official test.

Is this for IELTS Academic or General Training?

Our Writing and Speaking content is built specifically for IELTS Academic (Writing Task 1 asks you to describe a graph, chart, or table, not write a letter, as in General Training). Our Listening band conversion uses the same official scale IELTS applies to both Academic and General Training, so it's accurate either way. Our Reading band conversion currently only implements the Academic scale. General Training candidates can still use the same passages to practice reading skills, but the band shown won't accurately reflect real General Training scoring, which uses different, generally stricter thresholds at the same raw score. A dedicated General Training Reading scale is on our roadmap.

Do I need to create an account?

No sign-up required. Your progress is saved automatically in your browser. Just open the site and start practising.

Does it work on mobile?

Yes. ParrotTalk is mobile-friendly. The Speaking module requires microphone access, which most modern mobile browsers support. For the best AI feedback experience, we recommend Chrome or Firefox.

How often are new tests added?

We're actively expanding beyond the current 3 full mock tests per section. We don't have a fixed release schedule to commit to yet — check back on the site for new Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking tests.
